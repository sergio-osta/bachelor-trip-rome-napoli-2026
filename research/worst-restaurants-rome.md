**Last verified:** March 2026 (all entries checked for current operating status)

## Purpose

Find genuinely terrible tourist trap restaurants in Rome that can be presented to the groom as "hidden gems" or "must-try local spots." Maximum comedy value.

## Tier 1: Hall of Shame (internationally notorious scams)

### Antico Caffe di Marte
- **Location:** Via del Banco di Santo Spirito 7, near Castel Sant'Angelo / Vatican
- **What happened:** Two Japanese tourists charged **€430 for two plates of spaghetti, fish, and water**. Another visitor from Hong Kong charged **€600+** for pasta, fish, and water for two — and was **threatened by staff** when they protested. TripAdvisor had to **suspend the restaurant's review page**.
- **The scam:** Manager "suggests" a light fish dish, then serves a full seafood platter at market-rate-by-weight pricing nobody agreed to.
- **Prank pitch:** "Fresh fish right by the Vatican — a local secret!"
- **Warning:** This one is an actual scam. Only go here if you're prepared for the bill or want to walk out. Could genuinely cost serious money.

### S.P.Q.R. Restaurant
- **Location:** Via di Tor Millina, side street off Piazza Navona
- **The scam:** A group ordered four mains (€7-15 each), water, and house wine (quoted at €5). Accepted "bread and cheese, just a little, a few euros." Bill: **€190**. The bread was €40, the wine €60 (not €5), plus €17 service charge.
- **Prank pitch:** "Right off Piazza Navona — such a charming side street spot!"

### Trevi Cafe - Bar & Pizzeria
- **Location:** Near the Trevi Fountain
- **The scam:** Customer charged **€251 for three mains, three desserts, and drinks**, then restaurant **demanded €50 more in cash as mandatory tip**. Another customer paid €140.80 on card, then got a **separate handwritten bill for €40 more in cash**. Receipts show different restaurant names.
- **Warning:** Another actual scam operation. High risk of a bad scene.

### Da Carlos in Trevi
- **Location:** Meters from the Trevi Fountain
- **What's wrong:** "One of the worst restaurants I have seen as a worldwide traveller." Separate charges for items sold as a "special," aggressive tip-pushing, 10% surcharge on all items, poorly prepared seafood. The owner Carlos is specifically called out as rude.

### Alberto e Graziella
- **Location:** Vatican / Borgo area
- **What's wrong:** "The worst food in Rome" on TripAdvisor. Pizza, lasagne, salads all described as "crap." Aggressive bread upselling that inflates the bill.
- **Prank pitch:** "Classic family trattoria near the Vatican"

### Taverna Lino
- **Location:** Via Tunisi 18, near Vatican walls (Trionfale area)
- **What's wrong:** "Worst Italian food in Italy." Lasagne had "9 layers of pasta with only a drizzle of sauce and 20 grams of beef." House wine "tasted like ethanol with a hint of mouldy apple juice." 15% service charge. Worst pizza reviewer ever had in Rome.
- **Prank pitch:** "Authentic local taverna — the kind of place only real Romans know"

### La Taverna Trevi
- **Location:** Near the Trevi Fountain
- **What's wrong:** Classic tourist trap. Bland food, poor service, overpriced. Pizza described as "soggy and tasteless."

### Stendhal Roma
- **Location:** Galleria Alberto Sordi (NOT Via del Tritone 113 — that's a hotel)
- **What's wrong:** Overcooked eggplant "tough and terrible," tasteless sauce, chicken "dry as a bone." Ordered ossobuco with no risotto. Served one drink then ignored for 30 minutes.

## Tier 3: Overrated famous places (best for the prank)

These have name recognition, so you can sell them convincingly as "must-tries." They're not scams — just genuinely mediocre to bad food at inflated prices.

### Alfredo alla Scrofa
- **Location:** Via della Scrofa, Centro Storico (near Piazza Navona)
- **What's wrong:** The "original" fettuccine Alfredo restaurant — a dish that **is not a real Italian dish** (it's an American invention that Italians mock). Trades entirely on tourist fame. ~€200 for 3 fettuccine, 2 appetizers, 2 desserts, 2 beers. Overpriced, rude service.
- **Prank pitch:** "This is THE birthplace of Fettuccine Alfredo — we HAVE to go!" (technically true, maximum comedy)

### Tre Scalini
- **Location:** Piazza Navona 28/32
- **What's wrong:** Famous for "tartufo" chocolate dessert, but everything else is frozen/microwaved. €30 for a bad baba al rum, a "disgusting" tartufo, and two coffees. Broken glasses. Pressured by hawker on the street. Mussels and clams "unopened and inedible."
- **Prank pitch:** "You HAVE to try the world-famous tartufo at Piazza Navona!"

### Giggetto al Portico d'Ottavia (Da Giggetto)
- **Location:** Via del Portico d'Ottavia 21A, Jewish Ghetto
- **What's wrong:** Katie Parla (one of Rome's most respected food writers) called it **"abjectly awful" — "pure crap, deep fried in old oil"** using "agribusiness artichokes." In every tourist guidebook. Full of tourists, zero Romans.
- **Prank pitch:** "This has been in every guidebook for decades — the carciofi alla giudia here are legendary!"

### Pierluigi
- **Location:** Piazza de' Ricci 144, near Campo de' Fiori
- **What's wrong:** Celebrity-frequented seafood restaurant. Katie Parla found it "abundantly mediocre" — fish "swimming in a viscous pool of butter," "so-so pasta."
- **Prank pitch:** "This is where celebrities eat when they're in Rome!"

### Gusto
- **Location:** ~~Piazza Augusto Imperatore 9~~ → **MOVED to Piazza di S. Apollinare 41** (near Piazza Navona). Original location taken over by Bulgari Hotel.
- **What's wrong:** Katie Parla: "plain awful" food, staff "insufferably rude and inept." Concept "hasn't been interesting since the mid-1990s."
- **Note:** Reopened at new address. Unclear if quality changed with the move.

### Maccheroni
- **Location:** Piazza delle Coppelle 44, near the Pantheon
- **What's wrong:** Katie Parla: "very very bad." Prime tourist area, subpar cacio e pepe.
- **Prank pitch:** "Best cacio e pepe near the Pantheon — locals swear by it"

### Obika Mozzarella Bar
- **Location:** Via dei Prefetti 26a (Parlamento location — confirmed open). Campo de' Fiori location appears closed.
- **What's wrong:** Katie Parla: "if you love wasting money and hate food." Mozzarella "just ok," other food "terrible," "absolute worst service," staff "uninformed."
- **Prank pitch:** "This place specializes in THE freshest mozzarella in Rome!"

### Da Oio A Casa Mia
- **Location:** Testaccio
- **What's wrong:** "Eh" food for excessive prices. Cacio e pepe had "lumpy sauce with a kilo of butter." Once-good place that declined dramatically.

### Sora Margherita
- **Location:** Piazza delle Cinque Scole 30, Jewish Ghetto
- **What's wrong:** Katie Parla: "hasn't been good in years." Faded glory, mediocre food, tourists who read outdated guides.

## Tourist trap red flags (how to spot the worst)

1. **Hawker outside** — someone on the sidewalk trying to pull you in. 100% guarantee of tourist trap.
2. **Photo menus** — laminated menus with food photographs.
3. **6+ language menus** — designed for tourists, not food quality.
4. **Giant sidewalk menu displays** — billboard-size menus propped up outside.
5. **Margherita over €10** — real Roman pizzeria charges €6-8.
6. **Dinner service before 8 PM** — real Roman restaurants don't serve dinner at 5:30.
7. **Direct view of a famous landmark** — if you see the Colosseum from your table, the food is terrible.
8. **Names with "Gladiatore," "Antica," or "Hostaria"** — tourist-bait naming near the Colosseum.
9. **"Real spaghetti and meatballs"** — not a traditional Italian dish.
10. **"Grandma making pasta in the window"** — known tourist trap theater, especially in Trastevere.
