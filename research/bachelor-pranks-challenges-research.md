# Bachelor Pranks & Public Challenges — Full Research

**For:** Javi (the groom). Group of 12 lads.
**Where:** Rome (April 16) + Naples/Boscotrecase (April 17-19)
**Key asset:** Javi speaks Italian fluently.
**Goal:** Make Javi make a fool of himself in public. Fun, harmless, memorable.

---

## 1) Challenges that exploit Javi's Italian

These only work (or are much funnier) because Javi can actually talk to locals.

### The Convincer — approach strangers and commit with a straight face

- **Long-lost cousin:** approach an Italian in a piazza and insist passionately that you recognize them from a family reunion in Calabria in 2019. Double down when they deny it.
- **Directions to nowhere:** ask locals for directions to places that don't exist — "il Museo della Carbonara," "la Fontana delle Lacrime di San Gennaro," etc. Fully commit.
- **Non-existent products:** ask a shopkeeper for limoncello-flavored toothpaste, truffle-scented sunscreen, or gluten-free grappa.
- **Marriage crisis:** approach an Italian stranger and urgently ask for romantic advice — "I'm getting married next month and I'm having second thoughts, I need the wisdom of a Roman/Neapolitan man to guide me." The more dramatic, the better.
- **Haunted statue:** convince a local that a specific statue or fountain is haunted and that you saw it move. Ask if they've experienced it too.
- **Famous food blogger:** convince a waiter that you're a famous Spanish food blogger and need to take very specific photos. Ask them to hold a napkin "just so" for the lighting.

### The Negotiator — absurd haggling

- **Paperclip trade-up:** start with a single paperclip. 2 hours to trade up through swaps with strangers and shopkeepers, ending with the most valuable item possible. Every swap negotiated in Italian. Group follows and films.
- **Barter a sock for pizza:** try to trade a used sock for a slice of pizza at a takeaway window. Straight face.
- **Free espresso hustle:** haggle aggressively for a free espresso, claiming it's his birthday/wedding day/last day alive — escalating the backstory each attempt.
- **Gelato review committee:** negotiate a group discount at a gelateria for all 12, claiming you're a "professional gelato review committee" touring Italy.

### The Impersonator — pretend to be someone he's not

- **Fake sommelier:** at dinner, take over wine ordering. Elaborate swirl, sniff, hold to light, mutter about "terroir" and "tannin structure," send the first bottle back for an invented reason. Ask the waiter extremely specific questions about the vineyard.
- **Fake restaurant reviewer:** walk into a restaurant and ask to speak to the chef. Explain you're reviewing for an important Spanish food magazine. Ask very pretentious questions about their "philosophy of pasta."
- **Join a street performer:** approach a busker and ask (in Italian) if you can audition to join their act. Offer to be the "assistant."
- **Fake local giving directions:** station Javi near a landmark and have him confidently give directions to tourists. Subtly wrong but not harmful.

### Italian food crimes — commit the crime, deal with the reaction

Ranked by Italian outrage level (source: YouGov Italy survey):

1. **Ask for ketchup on pasta** (-82 score, maximum sin)
2. **Pineapple on pizza** (-63) — bonus: insist it's "a traditional recipe from my region"
3. **Cut spaghetti with scissors** (-46)
4. **Cream in carbonara** (-45) — ask "can you add a bit of cream?"
5. **Parmesan on seafood pasta** (-39) — "but my nonna always did it this way"
6. **Cappuccino after dinner** (-23) — defend the choice passionately
7. **Ask for "well done" steak** then request ketchup for it
8. **Order "pepperoni pizza"** — in Italy "peperoni" means bell peppers. Act confused when he gets peppers.

### Naples-specific (using Italian)

- **Ask a Neapolitan pizzaiolo what they think of Domino's.** Straight face. Film the reaction.
- **Ask for Hawaiian pizza at a serious Neapolitan pizzeria.**
- **Disagree with a Neapolitan about the offside rule.** Neapolitans are famously passionate about football. Ask someone to explain it, then disagree.
- **Ask a local to teach you Napoletano dialect.** Then use the words wrong in conversation with the next person.

## 2) Public performance & spectacle challenges

These work in any language but are elevated by the Italian piazza setting and tourist audiences.

### Piazza performances

- **Sing "O Sole Mio" in a public piazza** at full volume. Does not need to be good. Better if it's not.
- **Dramatic love poem:** hand Javi a piece of paper with an absurdly over-the-top love poem (written by the lads in advance) that he must read aloud in the piazza with maximum dramatic flair. Escalating ridiculous metaphors.
- **Start a conga line:** recruit at least 5 strangers in a piazza to join.
- **Teach strangers a dance move:** get at least 5 people to perform it simultaneously. The more complex and ridiculous the move, the better.
- **60-second TED Talk:** give a TED Talk to passing tourists on any topic. Must use hand gestures.
- **Duet with a street busker:** find a busker, ask if you can sing one song together. Film everything.

### Romantic spectacle

- **Propose to a stranger:** get down on one knee with a Haribo ring in a tourist area and propose to a random person.
- **Best man speech to strangers:** give an impromptu best man speech to a group of strangers in a bar or piazza, as if they're the wedding guests. Full emotion. Possibly tears.
- **Romeo declaration:** at the Spanish Steps or any scenic staircase, stand at the bottom and deliver a Romeo-style declaration of love to someone at the top.
- **Serenade a stranger with a love song.**

### Rome-specific performances

- **Gladiator challenge:** near the Colosseum, challenge a costumed gladiator to a thumb war or rock-paper-scissors duel. Give the "Are you not entertained?" speech.
- **Gladiator speech:** full Maximus Decimus Meridius monologue to passing tourists.
- **Papal wave walk:** near the Vatican, walk through the crowd doing a slow papal wave. Occasionally stop to "bless" people. When questioned: "I'm the new intern."

## 3) Rule-based challenges (sustained over a time period)

These run for a set period (1-2 hours) and create constant comedy.

- **The singing voice:** everything Javi says must be sung. Ordering at restaurants, asking for directions, talking to the group. All sung.
- **Yes Man rule:** Javi cannot say "no" to anything for a set period. The group exploits this.
- **Movie quote rule:** Javi can only speak in movie quotes. Can translate on the fly.
- **Italian Nonno hour:** must act as a stereotypical Italian grandfather for one hour — hands behind back, comment loudly on the weather and produce at every market stall, shake head disapprovingly at tourist behavior, offer unsolicited advice, complain coffee was better in his day, exaggerated hand gestures for everything.
- **Third person rule:** Javi must refer to himself in the third person for one hour. "Javi would like a pizza, please."
- **Compliment every stranger:** for 30 minutes, Javi must compliment every person he passes. In Italian.
- **Mr. President / Security Detail:** the group surrounds Javi in a Secret Service formation. Earpieces made of paper, sunglasses, muttering into wrists: "Eagle is moving." Javi has to play along.

## 4) Sell / barter / hustle challenges

- **Street seller:** fill a tray with random cheap items (novelty trinkets, candy, random objects from a euro store). Javi must sell them to tourists in a piazza. Profits go to the group drinks fund.
- **The busker:** give Javi a guitar (or just a hat on the ground). He must earn enough money busking to buy the group a round. Even if he can't play guitar — especially if he can't.
- **Clothing sale:** buy Javi a cheap t-shirt. He must sell pieces of it — tourists cut off whatever piece they want for a euro. Must pitch the sale in Italian.
- **Paperclip trade-up** (also listed above): start with a paperclip, trade up through swaps with strangers.

## 5) The sign on his back

**"HAI UNA SFIDA PER LO SPOSO? MANDA UN SMS A [number]"**
(Got a dare for the groom? Text this number)

Use the best man's phone number. Whenever a text comes in, Javi has to do whatever it says. Works brilliantly in Italy because the sign is in Italian so locals participate, and tourist areas mean tons of foot traffic. The group can also seed challenges by texting from their own phones at strategic moments.

English version for tourist-heavy areas: "DARE THE GROOM — TEXT [number]"

## 6) Costume / appearance challenges

- **Costume bait-and-switch:** tell Javi there's a costume theme for the day. He shows up in full costume. Nobody else is wearing one. He must wear it for the rest of the day.
- **Ridiculous accessory escalation:** every hour, add one more ridiculous accessory. By evening he's wearing a crown, a feather boa, novelty sunglasses, and a sash.
- **The wedding dress:** classic — Javi wears a cheap wedding dress/veil for part of the day. Tourist areas guarantee maximum visibility.
- **Matching outfits (but only Javi):** everyone secretly agrees on a normal outfit. Javi is given something that "matches the theme" but is wildly different.

## 8) Format & structure recommendations

### Option A: Dare card deck (recommended backbone)

Pre-print 15-20 cards in three tiers. Javi draws one every 30-60 minutes throughout the day.

| Tier | Points | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Green (easy)** | 1 pt | Get a selfie with a stranger wearing a hat. Ask someone for relationship advice. Sing Happy Birthday to a random person. Get a stranger to teach you a Napoletano word. |
| **Yellow (medium)** | 3 pts | Convince a stranger you're famous (2+ min conversation). Negotiate a free sample using only charm. Join a street performer for 30 seconds. Do 10 push-ups at a landmark. |
| **Red (hard)** | 5 pts | Trade a paperclip for something of value. Get a waiter to sing with you. Give a 60-second TED Talk to tourists. Convince 3 strangers to do a group photo with a ridiculous pose. |

Lowest score at end of trip buys a round of limoncello for everyone.

### Option B: Running photo scavenger hunt (parallel track)

Checklist that spans all days. Can run alongside the dare cards. Bonus points for completing themed sets (e.g., all Rome photos = bonus 5 points).

### Option C: Set pieces (pre-planned big moments)

2-3 coordinated group moments planted at specific times:
- The piazza serenade (everyone knows it's coming except Javi)
- The fake sommelier at dinner
- The trade-up challenge during an afternoon

### Option D: The sign

Works best for the Naples day out (April 18) when you're walking around the city for hours. Put it on in the morning, let dares roll in all day.

### Recommended combo

Use **dare cards as the daily backbone** + **photo scavenger hunt running in parallel** + **2-3 set pieces** planted at specific moments + **the sign** on the Naples day out.

## 9) What to avoid

- Nothing that could get anyone arrested or fined (no climbing monuments, no blocking traffic, no public indecency)
- Nothing that harasses people — if someone says no or looks uncomfortable, move on
- Nothing at religious sites (churches, Vatican interior) — keep it to piazzas and streets
- Nothing that damages property or cultural landmarks
- Nothing sexual/crude in public
- Don't mess with actual street vendors' livelihoods
- Don't impersonate police, officials, or clergy in a way that could cause real confusion
- Be aware of local laws on busking/selling — if approached by police, stop immediately and apologize
